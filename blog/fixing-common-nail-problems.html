Fixing Common Nail Problems

Having well-manicured nails is a confidence booster, but sometimes, unexpected problems arise. Whether itâ€™s chipped polish, weak nails, or stubborn stains, hereâ€™s how you can fix common nail problems and keep your nails looking flawless.

1. Fixing Chipped Nail Polish

Problem: Your freshly painted nails start chipping within a day or two. Solution

  • Always apply a base coat before painting your nails.
  • Use thin layers of nail polish instead of thick coats.
  • Seal the edges by running the brush along the tip of your nails.
  • Apply a top coat every two days to prolong the life of your manicure.

2. Strengthening Weak or Brittle Nails

Problem: Your nails break easily or feel too soft. Solution:

  • Use a nail hardener or strengthening polish.
  • Keep your nails hydrated with cuticle oil and hand cream.
  • Avoid excessive water exposure by wearing gloves when doing dishes.
  • Incorporate biotin-rich foods like eggs and nuts into your diet.

3. Dealing with Yellow or Stained Nails

Problem: Your nails appear yellowish due to frequent polish use. Solution:

  • Soak your nails in a mixture of lemon juice and baking soda for a few minutes.
  • Use a whitening toothpaste to gently scrub your nails.
  • Give your nails a break from dark-colored polishes.
  • Always apply a base coat to prevent staining.

4. Fixing Peeling Nails

Problem: Your nails are peeling and splitting into layers. Solution:

  • Moisturize your nails daily with vitamin E oil.
  • Avoid using acetone-based nail polish removers.
  • File your nails in one direction to prevent further peeling.
  • Eat foods rich in omega-3 fatty acids to improve nail health.

5. Preventing Hangnails and Dry Cuticles

Problem: Your cuticles are dry, and you frequently get painful hangnails. Solution:

  • Apply cuticle oil and massage it into your nails daily.
  • Use a gentle nail buffer to smooth out rough edges.
  • Never cut your cuticles too aggressively.
  • Keep your hands moisturized, especially in cold weather.
